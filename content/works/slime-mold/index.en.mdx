## Idea

The slime mold *Physarum polycephalum* has no brain, yet it finds the
shortest path through a maze. The secret is simple — it leaves a chemical on
its trail, and it moves toward where that chemical is strongest. This piece
recreates that behavior with **262,144 agents**.

## Three rules

Every frame, each agent:

1. **Senses** — reads the pheromone concentration at three points ahead
   (left, center, right)
2. **Turns** — steers toward the strongest one
3. **Steps** — moves one step forward, depositing pheromone where it stands

The pheromone map slightly spreads (diffuses) and fades (decays) each frame.
From this feedback loop alone — with no designer — a vein-like network
emerges.

## Core technique

The position and heading of all 262,144 agents live in a GPU texture, and
the pheromone map is a separate floating-point texture. One frame is a
ping-pong of four passes — agent update, pheromone deposit, diffuse-and-decay,
and render.

## Interaction

Move the cursor and pheromone is painted there. The whole network grows
toward the cursor. Requires WebGL2 and floating-point render targets.
