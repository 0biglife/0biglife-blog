## Idea

**1,216 draws of real winning data**, collected from the very first draw in
2002. Each of the 45 numbers becomes a star, and how often two were drawn
together becomes the light that links them. On this constellation you pick
six numbers yourself to draw *your combination*, and compare it against 24
years of winning records.

## Interaction

- **Hover a star** — it reveals how often (warm lines) or how rarely (cool
  lines) that number was drawn together with each of the others.
- **Click a star** — pick up to six. The lines joining your chosen stars
  are your combination.
- **With every pick** — the sum, odd/even split, low/high split, and
  consecutive count are instantly compared against the 1,216-draw
  distribution. Choose all six and it also checks whether that exact
  combination ever won, and how often the same shape appeared.
- **Click empty space** — reset.
- **Leave it alone** — real past winning draws play back quietly.

## What the data says

Pick six and you come to see it — **of 8,145,060 possible combinations, only
1,216 were drawn in 24 years**. No combination has ever appeared twice. And
yet the sum gathers into a bell curve, and odd/even leans toward 3:3. The
order that randomness draws.
