## Idea

Two virtual chemicals, A and B, **diffuse** and **react** on a grid. It is
just two simple rules, but iterated thousands of times, patterns resembling
coral, zebra stripes, and cell division grow on their own — the pattern
formation Alan Turing foresaw in 1952.

## Core technique

Each texel holds the chemical concentrations `(A, B)`, and the update shader
computes diffusion with a 3×3 Laplacian and reaction with a multiplicative
term. A ping-pong FBO carries the state forward, updating six times per
frame.

```glsl
float reaction = A * B * B;
float A2 = A + (1.0 * lapA - reaction + f * (1.0 - A));
float B2 = B + (0.5 * lapB + reaction - (k + f) * B);
```

Just two constants — `f` (feed rate) and `k` (kill rate) — decide the kind
of pattern; this demo uses coral-type values.

## Interaction

The cursor paints chemical B. Rub an empty area and a new pattern starts
spreading there. Requires WebGL2 and floating-point render targets.
